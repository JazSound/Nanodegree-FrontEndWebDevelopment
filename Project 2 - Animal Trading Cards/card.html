Slow Loris

While looking very adorable, the loris's teeth are actually quite venomous and the toxin on their teeth can also be applied to fur through grooming!

  • Genus: Nycticebus
  • Class: Mammalia
  • Lifespan: +/- 20 years
  • Habitat: southeastern Asia

Slow lorises have a round head, a narrow snout, large eyes, and a variety of distinctive coloration patterns that are species-dependent. Their arms and legs are nearly equal in length, and their trunk is long and flexible, allowing them to twist and extend to nearby branches. The hands and feet of slow lorises have several adaptations that give them a pincer-like grip and enable them to grasp branches for long periods of time.
